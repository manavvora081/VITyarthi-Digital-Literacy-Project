Stay Safe Online: Digital Security Checklist for Students

Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money: Remember that your secret UPI PIN is only ever required when sending funds or checking your bank balance.

Decline unknown payment requests: Immediately decline and report any unexpected "Request Money" prompts on your UPI apps.

Verify urgent requests: If a friend or family member asks for money urgently via WhatsApp or social media, call them directly to verify it's actually them and not a compromised account.

Avoid public Wi-Fi for banking: Never make online purchases or check your bank accounts while connected to open, unsecured campus or cafe Wi-Fi networks; use your mobile data instead.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Turn on 2FA for all critical accounts, especially your college email, banking apps, and primary social media profiles.

Beware of Phishing Links: Do not click on links in unsolicited SMS messages or emails claiming your bank account is "blocked" or a "package is delayed."

Keep software updated: Regularly update your phone's operating system and apps to ensure you have the latest security patches.

Report incidents immediately: If you fall victim to a cybercrime or financial fraud, report it immediately to the National Cyber Crime Portal or call the Helpline 1930 to freeze the fraudulent transaction.
