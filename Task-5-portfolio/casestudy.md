Case Study: UPI "Receive Money" Scam

What it is: UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a prevalent cybercrime in India where scammers trick individuals into transferring money by exploiting the "Request Money" feature on digital payment apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.

How it typically happens:

Initial Contact: The scammer contacts the victim, often posing as a buyer on a second-hand marketplace (like OLX), a customer care executive, or even a distant relative in urgent need.

The Hook: The scammer agrees to send money to the victim (e.g., to purchase a listed item or process a "refund").

The Trap: Instead of sending the money, the scammer initiates a "Request Money" prompt to the victim's UPI ID.

The Deception: The scammer calls the victim and pressures them to quickly open their UPI app and enter their PIN, falsely claiming that entering the PIN is required to receive the funds.

The Theft: Once the victim enters their secret UPI PIN, the requested amount is instantly deducted from their bank account and sent to the scammer.

Who is usually targeted: College students buying or selling used textbooks/laptops online, elderly individuals, and anyone who isn't entirely familiar with how the UPI transaction flow works.

Consequences: Victims suffer an immediate and often irreversible financial loss. For a college student, this could mean losing rent or tuition money, leading to severe emotional distress and a long-term loss of trust in digital payment systems.
