The 5 Do's: How to Behave Responsibly
Do review your privacy settings regularly: Ensure you know exactly who can see your posts, photos, and personal information across all platforms.
Do think before you post: Always remember the "Golden Rule" of the internet—once something is online, it is almost impossible to erase completely.
Do use platforms to build a positive brand: Share your academic milestones, volunteer work, and projects to create a professional footprint that future employers will appreciate.
Do verify information before sharing: Check the credibility of news or sensational posts before forwarding them to avoid spreading misinformation.
Do take regular digital detoxes: Balance your screen time with offline activities to protect your mental health and stay focused on your studies.

The 5 Don'ts: What to Avoid
Don't share sensitive personal details: Never post your home address, daily schedule, phone number, or financial information where strangers can see them.
Don't engage in cyberbullying or trolling: Avoid getting into heated, public arguments in comment sections or posting hurtful remarks about others.
Don't complain about academics publicly: Venting about professors, group project members, or your college can reflect poorly on your professionalism.
Don't accept requests from strangers: Be highly cautious about adding people you don't know in real life, as they could be using fake profiles for phishing or scams.
Don't post inappropriate content: Avoid sharing photos or language (including jokes in poor taste) that could jeopardize your future internship or job opportunities.
